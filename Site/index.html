Jane Eyre - Charlotte Brontë




Jane Eyre


“Jane Eyre”, Charlotte Brontë’s most known novel, was first published in 1847, under a male pseudonym because of the preconceptions of that period when it was abnormal for women to write. Built as an autobiography, it narrates the eponymous heroine’s evolution from childhood to adulthood, until she settled down. Brontë was called to be the “first historian of the private consciousness”, showing a special focus for the psychological and spiritual aspects of each character. Not only did she revolutionise prose fiction, but she also created a masterpiece that reflects British society from the nineteenth century, referring to family, marriage, education, religion, woman’s position.

This novel is a symbol of feminism, a creation that inspires readers to have a different approach towards life and its difficulties. It emphasizes the importance of having and respecting certain moral principles, while, at the same time, it encourages women to pursue their dreams and exceed the unfair norms imposed by society.
