Social aspects


Peasantry

Although people less fortunate or living in the rural areas had little access to education, many of them were kind, hard-workers, willing to learn more. Jane as well made this remark after teaching in Morton.

“I stood with the key in my hand, exchanging a few words of special farewell with some half-dozen of my best scholars: as decent, respectable, modest, and well-informed young women as could be found in the ranks of the British peasantry. And that is saying a great deal; for after all, the British peasantry are the best taught, best mannered, most self-respecting of any in Europe: since those days I have seen paysannes and Bäuerinnen; and the best of them seemed to me ignorant, coarse, and besotted, compared with my Morton girls.” (Charlotte Brontë, Jane Eyre, Chapter XXXIV)
