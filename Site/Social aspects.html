Jane Eyre - Charlotte Brontë




Social aspects


There are numerous writers from the Victorian era who had a great impact not only on people of that time, but also on the next generations. George Eliot, Thomas Hardy, Charles Dickens and, undoubtedly, Charlotte Brontë, they all described realistically throughout their novels the society from that period, referring to details such as the clothing, houses’ architecture, each person’s role in a family, the relationships between relatives.

Some extremely relevant aspects are the following:

  • Women's lives
  • Peasantry
  • Aristocrats and their Gatherings
  • Love and Marriage
  • Family Roles and Decisions
